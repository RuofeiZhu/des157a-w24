10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills

The article discusses the benefits of using engaging photos from The New York Times to encourage students to slow down and closely examine what is presented to them. By noticing small details and contemplating the events captured in the images, students can broaden their curiosity about the world and spark new ideas.

Strategies for Teachers

  • Interactive Detective Game: Transform the activity into a detective game where students attempt to unravel the story behind each photo.
  • Weekly Observations: Make it a weekly practice, such as every Monday, to develop their observational skills gradually.
  • Open-ended Questions: Pose questions that don't necessarily have right or wrong answers to encourage students to discuss their perspectives and interpretations.
  • Detail-focused Analysis: Prompt students to focus on minute details, aiding them in making more informed guesses about the story behind each photo.
  • Collaborative Insights: Encourage students to build upon the observations and ideas of their peers, fostering a deeper understanding and potentially altering initial perceptions.

Gonchar highlights that these activities are not only enjoyable but also cultivate essential skills applicable across various academic disciplines. For instance, students improve at substantiating their arguments with evidence, a crucial skill in both writing and scientific inquiry.
